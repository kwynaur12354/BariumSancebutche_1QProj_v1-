ABOUT HUNGER



Broad term of HUNGER

Hunger is a multifaceted global issue affecting millions, with 733 million people experiencing daily hunger despite sufficient global food production, and 2.8 billion unable to afford a healthy diet. It manifests in acute, chronic, and hidden forms, stemming from causes like natural disasters, poverty, conflict, inequality, and poor governance, exacerbated by climate change and biased global trade practices. The consequences create a vicious cycle of poverty, impacting physical and mental development, particularly among children, with malnutrition linked to half of all child deaths. However, uplifting stories from around the world demonstrate that acts of kindness and support from organizations and compassionate individuals can transform lives, providing hope and access to nutritious food, emphasizing the emotional and psychological dimensions of hunger and the power of empathy and community in addressing it.
