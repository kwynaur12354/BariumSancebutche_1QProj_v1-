What is HUNGER? Why is it considered as a GLOBAL problem?

Hunger is a state where a person lacks sufficient food for an active and healthy life, leading to malnutrition and health issues. It's considered a global problem because it affects millions worldwide, particularly in developing countries, hindering economic growth and social stability. Addressing hunger requires international cooperation and sustainable solutions to ensure food security for all. It is a complex issue with interconnected factors like poverty, conflict, and climate change.

Cause and Effects

Hunger and malnutrition have many causes, including natural disasters, poverty, wars and conflicts, inequality, biased global trade, poor governance, and waste of resources and climate change, and undernourishment. But, those are not only a consequence of poverty, it also causes poverty, by being passed on from generation to generation..

Statistics

In 2024, 733 million people (1 in 11) go hungry daily despite the world producing enough food; hunger is high in Africa (1 in 5), and 2.8 billion people can't afford a healthy diet. The Global Hunger Index ranks hunger as Serious in 36 countries and Alarming in 6, with the number of hungry people rising by 152 million since 2019, and malnutrition linking to half of all child deaths.
